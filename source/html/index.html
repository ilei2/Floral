Sunflowers

Characterized by their bright yellow petals, sunflowers have been admired for its sunny charm and beautiful disposition. Sunflower oil is used for many purposes, from cooking to medicine to personal care.
Close

Roses

Rose plants range in size and shape and colors can range from white, yellow, and red hues. They are widely known for their beauty and fragrance. They are popular in culture and are often used a symbol of love.
Close

Iris

Iris is derived from the Greek word for rainbow. Its shape was adapted on royal banners, such as the "Fleur de Lys". The Iris also has medicinal properties and is often used for stomach problems.
Close

Poppy

Poppies are often used as a symbol for peace, sleep, and death. Opium extracted from poppies are used as sedatives, and red poppies are often used to remember those who have died during wartime.
Close

Cape Town Blue

A native species from South Africa, this blue daisy is a favorite since it is low-maintenance and blooms all season. Since blue is an unusual color to have in a garden, Cape Town Blue is definitely a plant that will draw attention to the eye!
Close

Lily

The flower is so revered by the ancient Greeks that they believed that it sprouted from the milk of Hera, the queen of the Gods. The colors of the lily symbolize different things: white represents virtue, and other hues represent friendship, wealth, or prosperity.
Close

Gerbera Daisy

Discovered in South Africa, Gerbera daisies come in vibrant colors, including yellows, snow white, and ruby red hues. Gerberas are often synonymous with bright and merry, and are a favorite choice for birthdays, graduations, and congratulation events.
Close

Azalea

Azaleas are popular landscaping flowers, and are often used in centerpieces and bouquets. The flower symbolizes a reminder of the time spent at home.
Close

Gordon Cooper

Also known as rock rose, Gordon Coopers are recognizable by its white petals with a deep maroon spot at the base of each petal. The flower is drought-tolerant, making it a good choice to plant in dry climates.
